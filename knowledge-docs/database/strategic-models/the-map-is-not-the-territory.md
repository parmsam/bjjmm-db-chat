## **Mental models are not always 100% accurate.**

"The map is not the territory" is a mental model coined by [Alfred Korzybski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Korzybski). It tells us that **even the best mental models have exceptions, and we shouldn't rely on them dogmatically.**

[Shane Parrish from Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/2015/11/map-and-territory/) does an excellent job explaining this mental model:

> When we see a powerful model work well, we tend to over-apply it, using it in non-analogous situations. We have trouble delimiting its usefulness, which causes errors.
> 
> [—Shane Parrish](https://fs.blog/2015/11/map-and-territory/)

Much like how a map does not perfectly and fully represent the territory it depicts, mental models do not fully and accurately represent every single use case. Mental models are powerful learning and decision-making tools, but **very few of them apply 100% of the time.**It takes wisdom, experience, and humility to identify when the rules don't apply and it's time to think outside the box.
